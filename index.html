The VRP Mechanism

When VRP < 0 (options cheap relative to realized variance), the risk-neutral density assigns less probability to extreme outcomes than the physical distribution. This causes RND VaR to be systematically too low — producing violation rates of 2.25–2.71%, more than double the nominal 1%.

GLD's positive VRP partially insulates it — investors pay a premium for gold downside protection, keeping RND more conservative. This produces GLD's lowest violation rate (2.25%) among the four ETFs.

Forward-looking ≠ faster in sudden crashes.
GARCH's backward-looking feedback responds same-day. Options require market repricing time.

Methodology

Why GARCH-EVT Wins

The answer lies in one structural asymmetry between the two model families.

⚡

Immediate Volatility Feedback

GARCH σ²ₜ absorbs ε²ₜ₋₁ on the same day as the shock. VaR spikes immediately when realized losses hit. No repricing lag. No market friction.

📉

VRP Creates Structural Bias

When VRP < 0 (IWM, QQQ, TLT), option prices embed a discount relative to realized variance. RND systematically underestimates physical crash risk — producing 2.25–2.71% violation rates vs nominal 1%.

🔍

SHAP Confirms the Driver

Random Forest + SHAP attribution across 9,037 observations confirms GARCH σ(t) is the dominant exogenous spread driver in every asset class and every market regime. R² gap of 0.286 validates non-linear RF+SHAP over linear OLS.

"Forward-looking does not mean faster." In the COVID-19 crash, GARCH's backward-looking volatility feedback responded same-day while the options market required repricing time. This holds across SPX, GLD, IWM, QQQ, and TLT.
